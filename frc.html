What is FIRST Robotics Competition?

FRC (FIRST Robotics Competition) is one of the world's most prestigious robotics competitions, organized for high school students to develop their skills in engineering, software, design, and teamwork. Each year, an international theme and mission are announced; teams design robots from scratch to fulfill this mission, build them with mechanical and electronic systems, code them, and develop strategies.

The competition focuses not only on technical knowledge but also on innovation, problem-solving, time management, and team coordination. Participants get the opportunity to experience real engineering processes and interact directly with many professional engineers, companies, and universities.

However, FRC is not just a robotics competition; with its "More than Robots" motto, it emphasizes values of contributing to society, leadership, and inspiration. Teams strive to inspire young people in STEM (Science, Technology,Engineering, and Mathematics) fields, carry out social responsibility projects, and spread innovative thinking.

The concepts of "Gracious Professionalism" and "Coopertition" (cooperation while competing), which form the spirit of the competition, ensure that FRC is not only a technical challenge but also an arena for character, leadership, and solidarity.
